Our Story

From a family kitchen to your table

It started with one recipe

In 2018, Chef Arjun Mehta left his corporate job to pursue something he'd loved since childhood — cooking the dishes his grandmother taught him. What started as a weekend pop-up in a friend's cafe turned into a 60-seat restaurant within two years.

Every dish at Saffron Kitchen is rooted in traditional Indian cooking but presented with a modern touch. We source our spices directly from farms in Kerala and Rajasthan, and our menu changes with the seasons to keep things fresh.

We're not trying to reinvent Indian food. We're trying to serve it the way it was meant to be — with care, good ingredients, and no shortcuts.

What We Believe In

Fresh Ingredients

We source locally wherever possible. Our vegetables come in every morning, and our spices are ground in-house.

No Shortcuts

Our dal simmers for 8 hours. Our marinades sit overnight. Good food takes time and we don't rush it.

Everyone's Welcome

Whether it's a weeknight dinner or a celebration, we want every guest to feel at home here.
